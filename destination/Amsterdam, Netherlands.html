Amsterdam, Netherlands

The vibe in the Dutch capital is arguably the most relaxed of any city in Europe. You may travel to Amsterdam with confidence that it will be one of your most memorable experiences. The city has everything, including a thriving nightlife, relaxing picnic areas, and some outstanding museums. You'll have a tonne of options to choose from!

Plan your next trip to Amsterdam with FHC Deals.

By Air

Schiphol International Airport is located 15 kilometers southwest of the main city. Regular flights connect it to many locations across the globe. KLM is the main airline of the Netherlands, however Air France, British Airways, Emirates, Lufthansa, Singapore Airlines, and US Airways all fly out of this airport. You can easily take a public bus or rent a cab to reach your accommodation from the airport.

By Rail

Excellent train connections connect Amsterdam with the majority of other European cities. High-speed trains run from London, Brussels, Paris, Frankfurt, and Berlin to Amsterdam. The advantages of taking the train to the Dutch capital include getting to see the beautiful countryside. It is advised that you go to Amsterdam on the Eurostar from London (changing in Brussels), which can be done in less than 7 hours.

Things to do and places to see

Van Gogh Museum

Like its name would imply, the Van Gogh museum is home to a large collection of the late Dutch artist's artwork, including drawings, paintings, and letters. Since its opening in 1973, it has undergone numerous improvements to be constantly modern. The museum only displays Vincent Van Gogh's and his contemporaries' works. The structure is situated in the southern region of the city at Museum Square. Since it contains nearly 200 of Vincent Van Gogh's paintings, essays, and illustrations, it draws art fans from all over the world.

The museum is essentially a study of the life of the artist. Everything from his letters to his artwork to the sources of his inspiration is on exhibit. To be more precise, there were over 200 paintings, 500 drawings, and 700 letters. Inside the museum, research facilities have been constructed in an effort to examine every facet of his life as well as the evolution of his aesthetic preferences.

Canal Cruise

The world-famous canal tours in Amsterdam are a stunning way to see the city. You travel through the center of the city on the ship, which is an educational and entrancing experience.

The city's 17th-century canal system is one really fascinating way to explore the area. It is a distinctive method to explore the city and its sights because you are merely floating through it. It is breathtaking to see when the fair lights along the belt turn on at night. The boats can be used for hop-on, hop-off sightseeing excursions as well as the one-hour sightseeing cruise. You can even reserve a candlelit meal for a memorable evening.

Rijksmuseum

In Amsterdam's Museumplein, the magnificent Rijksmuseum is home to approximately 8000 works of art and historical artifacts. The collections contain works of art by well-known painters like Rembrandt, Vermeer, and Frans Hals. The State Museum, or Rijksmuseum in English, has been a fixture in the city for more than 200 years. Art lovers flock to the museum to see masterpieces like Vermeer's "The Milkmaid" and Rembrandt's ‘Night Watch’. The museum's grounds include a lovely garden area where visitors can unwind, take in the sculptures, and take in the temporary exhibitions that are held there in the spring and fall.

Vondelpark

Spanning across 120 acres, Vondelpark is the biggest and most well-liked park in Amsterdam. Joost van den Vondel, a poet and playwright from the 17th century, is honored by the name of this attraction, which welcomes over 10 million tourists annually. The park is a perfect place for recreation as well as for tourist purposes with its open-air theater, children’s playground and cafeterias. People do a variety of things, like exercise, cycle, sit, read, walk their dogs, listen to music, or just relax. On weekends in particular, free concerts are presented in the outdoor theater, and the café offers delectable refreshments.

Beer Tasting

The culture of beer tasting and locally brewed beer are well known in Amsterdam. The city is home to numerous microbreweries that serve a wide range of beers, from dark and bitter to fruity and white.

Amsterdam is just as much of a beer lover's paradise as is Belgium. The locally produced beers here are excellent and offer a great selection. One of the most well-known places to sample beers is the brewery Brouwerij 'T Ij, which is located at the base of the Aede Gooyer windmill. In addition, Twee Zwaantjes and Wynand Fockink notice a crowd of individuals. If you can't visit the neighborhood breweries, 't Arendsnest is one pub that will let you sample a variety of Dutch beers.

Where to stay

Grachtengordel

The Grachtengordel neighborhood is expanding and is perfectly situated for the abundance of clubs, pubs, and restaurants on and around Leidseplein en Rembrandtplein. The Anne Frank House, a must-see in Amsterdam with or without children, is also located there.

For those on a tighter budget, there are also lots of options, including a handful of quiet charming - and even fashionable - hotels along the neighborhood canals.

Jordaan

When you stay in Jordaan, you are among the inhabitants and far from the popular tourist destinations. There are plenty of bars and eateries in this area, and some of the city's most picturesque canals run through it, but you'll still be at least 15 minutes' walk from the city's neon lights.

Be mindful that Marnixstraat and Rozengracht are congested main thoroughfares when seeking for a place to stay.

The Museum Quarter

The Van Gogh Museum and the Rijksmuseum are located in the Museum Quarter, which is the ideal place to stay in Amsterdam for cultural attractions. The nightlife in the Leidseplein district is also conveniently close by.

There are no canals in this area, and two of the major thoroughfares are continuously congested with traffic, but the neighborhood has a number of high-quality hotels.

Best time to visit

The year round appeal of Amsterdam. However, the main tourist season is between May and August when the weather is at its best. To help you decide when to visit, here is a breakdown of Amsterdam's weather by month: May to August: Amsterdam experiences pleasant weather during this time.

The best time to visit Amsterdam is between April and May or September and November – right before or directly after the summertime high tourist season.

Amsterdam has an oceanic climate strongly influenced by its proximity to the North Sea to the west, with prevailing westerly winds.

Amsterdam, as well as most of the North Holland province, lies in USDA Hardiness zone 8b. Frosts mainly occur during spells of easterly or northeasterly winds from the inner European continent. Even then, because Amsterdam is surrounded on three sides by large bodies of water, as well as having a significant heat-island effect, nights rarely fall below -5 °C (23 °F), while it could easily be -12 °C (10 °F) in Hilversum, 25 km (16 mi) southeast.

Summers are moderately warm with a number of hot and humid days with occasional rain every month.
